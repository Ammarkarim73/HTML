About Biryani

Biryani also known as biriyani, biriani, birani or briyani, is a South Asian mixed rice dish with its origins among the Muslims of the Indian subcontinent. It is popular throughout the Indian subcontinent and among the diaspora from the region. It is generally made with spices, rice and meat (chicken, mutton, beef, prawn or fish). Biryani is a rice-based dish made with spices, rice (usually basmati), and mixed with chicken, mutton etc. Biryani was believed to have been invented in the kitchen of Mughal Emperors. It is very popular in the Indian subcontinent and is a key element of the South Asian cuisine.
